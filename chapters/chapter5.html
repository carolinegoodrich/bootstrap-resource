Chapter 5:

Our Final Project, Part 1

Since we live in a consumer-driven society and are slaves to the industry, we’re going to develop a product branding page for our final project!

Our first step in this process is to brainstorm. My go-to is the good ol’ fashioned pen and paper method. Take a few minutes to jot down a product, either made up or real (maybe even yourself?! I mean this in a strictly professional manner) and write out 3 aspects you would like to focus on. For example, if I was creating a page to advertise Manatee’s, I would use what they are, where they live, and their dwindling numbers as focus points.

This project is going to be separated into two sections. The first being the HTML coding, and the second being the styling.We’re also going to set up this project as a multi-page site. Let’s get started!

Step 1:

Create another folder (I know, I’m sorry) in the same location as the other folders you’ve created for this tutorial, just to keep everything organized. Inside, create a CSS folder and an IMG folder. This helps keep all of your files organized and is an industry standard.

Step 2:

Following along with my Manatee example, let’s create some of our pages, choose a word that is descriptive but also unique to that focus point. You should have an index.html page always. These are the names of my pages, be creative and specific when you create yours!

  1. index.html
  2. who.html
  3. what.html
  4. where.html

You can add more pages, if you like as well.

Step 3:

We are going to create two “templates” for our pages. First being an engaging and creative index page that is unique, and the second will be a skeleton for your additional information pages. This will give you experience in working with one stylesheet being applied to multiple pages, and using consistent tags for consistent styling.

So let’s get started with our index.html file. We’re going to start by incorporating components whose code can be copied directly from the getbootstrap site. I know you may consider this cheating, but literally everyone in computing copies off of eachother. If you don’t believe me, visit the getbootstrap site and look at how many sites use the same template! We can always tweak and change it to suit our needs. This saves us time so we can spend it doing things we enjoy, such as going for hikes, or reading books, or drinking wine and crying in the shower.

Step 4:

Our home page is going to have 3 basc components:

  1. A navigation bar (http://getbootstrap.com/components/#navbar)
  2. three images that serve as quick descriptions of the other pages on the site
  3. a footer with additional information (contact, copyright, logo, etc.)

Step 5:

Our template for the pages is going to have 3 basic components, as well:

  1. The same nav bar that is used on the index, so it can be used across the site.
  2. An image banner that mirrors the image on the home page.
  3. A creative header title and a few paragraphs about what we’re trying to sell.

Your pages can have more components, and by all means, please do! Like last time, please download and use my project files as a reference. We will add styling and transform our pages in the next chapter!
